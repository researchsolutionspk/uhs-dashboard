Study at a Glance

A school-based endline survey evaluating the Empowering Girls programme across urban schools in Pakistan. The study measures changes in gender norms, safety perceptions, academic aspirations, and psychological wellbeing among Grade 8 students — with separate instruments for boys and girls using validated psychometric scales and Randomised Response Technique (RRT) for sensitive items.

Methodology: Each endline girl's b_reg_id and new_reg_id are checked against Table 1 / Table 2 IDs extracted from her school's Sample Tables docx file. Enumerator misclassifications (e.g., a baseline girl logged as "new" on the device) are auto-corrected by this roster match — the docx is treated as the source of truth.
Coverage expands automatically: Drop new .docx files into Sample/Sample Tables/ and re-run python generate.py. Schools without a roster file contribute to totals but not to the buckets above — their endline rows become countable as soon as a roster is added.

Sample design: Girls Treatment schools = 20 baseline+training + 20 training-only girls per school (total 40); Girls Control schools = 20 baseline girls per school; Boys Treatment schools = trained boys only (no baseline). Boys Control schools have no baseline or training and therefore no coverage breakdown. ID matching uses normalised strings against both b_reg_id (baseline-flow) and new_reg_id (new-flow) columns in the endline CSV.
